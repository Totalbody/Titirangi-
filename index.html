Frequently Asked Questions

What is an ACC injury?
An ACC injury refers to an injury covered by the Accident Compensation Corporation in New Zealand. This includes injuries from accidents at work, home, or during sports activities. ACC provides support for treatment and rehabilitation of these injuries.
Do I need a referral to see a physiotherapist?
No, you don't need a referral to see a physiotherapist. You can book directly with us. However, if you're seeking treatment for an ACC-covered injury, you may need a referral from your GP or specialist.
How long does a typical physiotherapy session last?
A typical physiotherapy session at Total Body Physio lasts between 30 to 60 minutes, depending on the nature and complexity of your condition. Initial consultations may take longer as they include a comprehensive assessment.
What should I wear to my physiotherapy appointment?
We recommend wearing comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated. For lower body issues, shorts or loose trousers are ideal. For upper body concerns, a tank top or loose shirt works well.
How many sessions will I need?
The number of sessions required varies depending on your specific condition, its severity, and how your body responds to treatment. After your initial assessment, your physiotherapist will provide a treatment plan and an estimate of the number of sessions you might need.
