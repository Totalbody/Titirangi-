About Us

Total Body Physio has been serving the Titirangi community since 2004, offering a wide range of physiotherapy services.

Total Body Physio has been a cornerstone of health and wellness in the Titirangi community since 2004. Our journey began with a simple yet powerful goal: to provide exceptional physiotherapy care that truly makes a difference in people's lives.

Over the years, we've grown not just in size, but in the depth and quality of care we offer. Our growth has always been driven by our desire to help more people, not simply to increase profits. We believe that by expanding our reach, we can positively impact more lives and contribute to the overall health and wellbeing of our community.

At Total Body Physio, we pride ourselves on our hands-on approach. We combine expert manual therapy with personalized exercise programs, always focusing on the individual needs of each patient. Our style is complemented by what we call our 'best practices' - a blend of evidence-based techniques and our collective experience that ensures the highest standard of care.

We're not just physiotherapists; we're partners in your health journey. Our team's personality shines through in every interaction, creating a warm, welcoming environment where healing and improvement flourish. We're committed to continual learning and improvement, always striving to bring the latest and most effective physiotherapy techniques to our practice.

Choose Total Body Physio for care that goes beyond treatment - we're here to educate, support, and empower you on your path to optimal physical health.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an ACC injury?
An ACC injury refers to an injury covered by the Accident Compensation Corporation in New Zealand. This includes injuries from accidents at work, home, or during sports activities. ACC provides support for treatment and rehabilitation of these injuries.
Do I need a referral to see a physiotherapist?
No, you don't need a referral to see a physiotherapist. You can book directly with us. However, if you're seeking treatment for an ACC-covered injury, you may need a referral from your GP or specialist.
How long does a typical physiotherapy session last?
A typical physiotherapy session at Total Body Physio lasts between 30 to 60 minutes, depending on the nature and complexity of your condition. Initial consultations may take longer as they include a comprehensive assessment.
What should I wear to my physiotherapy appointment?
We recommend wearing comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated. For lower body issues, shorts or loose trousers are ideal. For upper body concerns, a tank top or loose shirt works well.
How many sessions will I need?
The number of sessions required varies depending on your specific condition, its severity, and how your body responds to treatment. After your initial assessment, your physiotherapist will provide a treatment plan and an estimate of the number of sessions you might need.
